Experience Kerala Culture

Arts

Kathakali

A classical dance-drama known for elaborate costumes, face makeup, and expressive gestures (mudras). Stories are mainly from the Ramayana and Mahabharata. It combines dance, music, acting, and martial movements. Performed traditionally in temples and cultural festivals.

Mohiniyattam

A graceful classical dance performed mainly by women. Movements are soft, circular, and feminine. Themes are devotional and romantic, mostly dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Costume includes white and gold saree with simple makeup.

Krishnanattam

Temple art dedicated to Lord Krishna. Based on the life story of Krishna. Performed in Guruvayur Temple. Uses vibrant costumes and devotional music.

Kalaripayattu

Ancient martial art of Kerala. Includes combat techniques, weapons, and body training. Practiced in Kalari (training arena). Believed to be one of the oldest martial arts.

Kerala Mural Painting

Traditional temple wall paintings. Uses natural colors and mythological themes. Seen in temples and palaces. Follows strict style and proportions.

Nettipattam

Decorative golden ornament used for temple elephants. Used during temple festivals like Thrissur Pooram. Made with metal and embellishments. Symbol of Kerala temple culture.

Coir Craft

Made from coconut fiber. Produces ropes, mats, carpets, and handicrafts. Major cottage industry in Kerala. Exported worldwide.

Aranmula Mirror

Unique metal mirror made in Aranmula. Made from special metal alloy, not glass. Traditional craft with GI tag. Used as decorative item.

Festivals

Kerala New Year festival celebrated in April. Vishukkani (auspicious first sight) is important ritual. Fireworks and giving Vishukkaineetam (money) to children. Related Folk Arts: Kolkali and local temple folk dances during celebrations.

Thrissur Pooram

Famous temple festival held in Thrissur. Known for decorated elephants and fireworks. Includes traditional percussion performances. Related Folk Arts: Kolkali and other temple folk performances during festivities.

Beautiful village near Vembanad Lake. Known for bird sanctuary and houseboats. Coconut trees and traditional wooden houses. Fishing and tourism are main livelihoods.

Coastal fishing village. Calm beaches and simple village homes. Traditional fishing using small boats and nets. Famous for coir making and coconut farming.

Poovar

Coastal-backwater village. Fishing and coconut farming are main occupations. Traditional wooden boats and calm waterways. Beautiful sunset views and village tourism.
